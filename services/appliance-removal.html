What We Remove

Appliances We Haul Away

If it plugs in or runs on gas and you don't want it anymore, there's a good chance we can take it. Here's what we commonly remove.

Refrigerators & Freezers

All sizes — mini fridges, full-size refrigerators, chest freezers, and upright freezers. Refrigerant handled properly.

Washers & Dryers

Top-load, front-load washers, electric and gas dryers — we disconnect and remove them from laundry rooms of any size.

Stoves & Ovens

Freestanding gas and electric ranges, wall ovens, and cooktops. We safely disconnect before removal.

Dishwashers

Built-in or portable dishwashers — we disconnect the water line and haul them out cleanly.

Water Heaters

Tank and tankless water heaters, electric or gas — drained and removed from utility rooms, garages, or closets.

Air Conditioners

Window AC units, portable ACs, and through-wall units. Florida heat means everyone has one — we take them when they go.

Microwaves

Countertop, over-the-range, and built-in microwaves — including removal from cabinetry if needed.

Dehumidifiers & Air Purifiers

Whole-home and portable dehumidifiers, air purifiers, and humidifiers you no longer use.

Other Large Appliances

Wine coolers, trash compactors, ice makers, garage refrigerators — if it's large and runs on power, we'll take it.

Eco-note: Appliances containing refrigerants (fridges, freezers, AC units) require certified disposal. We handle this the right way — no illegal dumping, no shortcuts. Call us at (727) 810-4816 with any questions about a specific item.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you disconnect the appliance?
We can disconnect most standard appliances (washers, dryers, fridges) at pickup. For hardwired or gas connections, a licensed tech must disconnect first.
Do you recycle old appliances?
Yes. Metal and working parts are recycled whenever possible, and refrigerants are handled according to EPA guidelines.
Is there an extra fee for fridges or freezers?
Refrigerators and freezers involve refrigerant handling, so pricing reflects that — but there are no hidden fees. You’ll know the total before we load.
Can you take just one appliance?
Absolutely. Single-item pickups are welcome.
